That is because the high density molecular tracers have not been detected in NGC 205. Also, Welch et al. (1998) have attempted to estimate the strength of the interstellar UV field at the positions of the molecular clouds in NGC 205 by counting the known bright blue stars and estimating the UV field produced by the old stellar population. They estimate that the UV field in NGC 205 is about one tenth as strong as the standard solar neighborhood field. Thus, I speculate that a lower UV field in NGC 205 makes it easier to form and retain molecular gas in that galaxy than in the disk of our own Galaxy.
